# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

Taqdimah is built as welfare and coordination infrastructure for the Ummah. The project should reflect adab, sincerity, truthfulness, mercy, and practical benefit.

Everyone participating in this project is expected to help keep the community respectful, useful, and safe.

## Expected Behavior

- Speak with adab, even when disagreeing.
- Assume good intent, but correct mistakes clearly.
- Keep discussions focused on what is beneficial, truthful, halal, and practical.
- Protect privacy, especially around zakat, welfare, family, legal, financial, or hardship matters.
- Give credit for ideas, code, research, and design work.
- Welcome contributors at different skill levels.
- Separate product discussion from religious rulings unless qualified people are involved.
- Prefer evidence, careful reasoning, and humility over argument.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, insults, threats, or intimidation.
- Sectarian hostility, takfir, inflammatory religious argument, or public shaming.
- Discrimination based on religion, ethnicity, nationality, gender, disability, age, or background.
- Sharing private information without permission.
- Exploiting welfare, zakat, hardship, or security information.
- Spam, scams, deceptive fundraising, or promotion of impermissible activity.
- Repeated off-topic comments after maintainers ask for focus.
- Presenting unqualified religious, legal, tax, medical, or financial advice as authoritative.

## Privacy and Sensitive Topics

Do not post private hardship cases, zakat requests, family disputes, identity documents, phone numbers, addresses, financial records, or security vulnerabilities in public discussions or issues.

If a matter is sensitive, keep public posts minimal and ask maintainers where to send private details.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take action when behavior harms the project or community. Actions may include:

- A private reminder.
- Editing or hiding comments.
- Moving a conversation to a better place.
- Locking a discussion or issue.
- Temporary or permanent removal from participation.
- Reporting abuse to GitHub when needed.

Enforcement should be fair, proportionate, and focused on protecting the community and the mission.

## Reporting

If you see behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, contact the maintainers privately when possible. If private contact is not available, open a short issue or discussion asking for a maintainer contact without sharing sensitive details.

For security vulnerabilities, follow [SECURITY.md](./SECURITY.md).
